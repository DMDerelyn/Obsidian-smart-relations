# D&D 5e Combat Mechanics

Combat in Dungeons & Dragons 5th Edition follows a structured turn-based system that balances tactical depth with narrative flexibility. Understanding these mechanics is essential for both players and Dungeon Masters.

## Initiative

When combat begins, every participant rolls **initiative** to determine the order of turns. Each creature rolls a d20 and adds their Dexterity modifier. The DM may also apply situational modifiers for surprise or environmental factors.

- Ties are broken by comparing Dexterity scores
- Some features like the Alert feat grant bonuses to initiative
- The *War Magic* tradition and *Swashbuckler* subclass offer unique initiative mechanics

## Attack Rolls

To make an attack, a creature rolls a **d20** and adds the relevant modifiers:

1. **Melee attacks**: Add Strength modifier + proficiency bonus (if proficient)
2. **Ranged attacks**: Add Dexterity modifier + proficiency bonus
3. **Spell attacks**: Add spellcasting ability modifier + proficiency bonus

A roll of **20** is a critical hit, automatically hitting and doubling damage dice. A roll of **1** is an automatic miss regardless of modifiers.

### Advantage and Disadvantage

Instead of stacking numerical bonuses, 5e uses the #advantage-disadvantage system. When you have advantage, roll two d20s and take the higher result. Disadvantage means rolling two and taking the lower. These cancel each other out regardless of how many sources apply.

## Armor Class (AC)

**Armor Class** represents how hard a creature is to hit effectively. The base AC calculation depends on armor type:

| Armor Type | AC Calculation |
|------------|----------------|
| Unarmored | 10 + DEX modifier |
| Light Armor | Armor base + DEX modifier |
| Medium Armor | Armor base + DEX modifier (max +2) |
| Heavy Armor | Armor base (no DEX) |

Shields add +2 to AC. Some class features like *Unarmored Defense* provide alternative calculations.

## Actions in Combat

On each turn, a creature can take one **action**, one **bonus action**, one **reaction** (per round), and movement up to their speed. Common actions include:

- **Attack**: Make one or more weapon attacks
- **Cast a Spell**: Use a spell with a casting time of 1 action
- **Dodge**: Impose disadvantage on attacks against you
- **Dash**: Double your movement for the turn
- **Disengage**: Move without provoking opportunity attacks
- **Help**: Grant an ally advantage on their next check or attack

Understanding the action economy is crucial for effective #combat-tactics in 5e encounters.
