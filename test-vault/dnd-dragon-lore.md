# Dragon Lore in Dungeons & Dragons

Dragons are among the most iconic and powerful creatures in D&D. They are intelligent, ancient beings with distinct personalities, hoards, and territorial behaviors. The classification system divides them into several major families.

## Chromatic Dragons

Chromatic dragons are typically **evil-aligned** and serve Tiamat, the five-headed dragon queen. Each color corresponds to a different breath weapon and personality:

- **Red Dragons**: Fire breath. The most arrogant and greedy of all dragons. They lair in volcanic mountains and collect vast hoards of gold. Challenge Rating ranges from 10 (young) to 24 (ancient).
- **Blue Dragons**: Lightning breath. Cunning tacticians who burrow beneath desert sands. They are vain and territorial, often dominating lesser creatures.
- **Green Dragons**: Poison breath. Master manipulators who prefer forests and intrigue over direct combat. They delight in corrupting others.
- **Black Dragons**: Acid breath. Cruel and sadistic, dwelling in swamps and marshes. They enjoy watching their prey suffer.
- **White Dragons**: Cold breath. The least intelligent chromatic dragons, driven by primal instinct. They hunt in arctic regions.

## Metallic Dragons

Metallic dragons are generally **good-aligned** and serve Bahamut, the Platinum Dragon. They possess two breath weapons each:

- **Gold Dragons**: Fire breath and weakening breath. The most powerful and wise, often taking humanoid forms to walk among mortals.
- **Silver Dragons**: Cold breath and paralyzing breath. Friendly and curious about humanoid cultures, frequently befriending adventurers.
- **Bronze Dragons**: Lightning breath and repulsion breath. Drawn to conflict and justice, they often aid coastal communities.
- **Copper Dragons**: Acid breath and slowing breath. Jovial tricksters who love riddles and jokes.
- **Brass Dragons**: Fire breath and sleep breath. Sociable and talkative, sometimes trapping travelers in lengthy conversations.

## Dragon Lairs and Lair Actions

Ancient and legendary dragons reshape the environment around their lairs through sheer magical presence. These **lair actions** occur on initiative count 20 and can include:

- Tremors and cave collapses
- Magma eruptions or pools of acid
- Grasping vegetation or illusory terrain
- Regional effects extending miles from the lair

## Breath Weapons

A dragon's breath weapon recharges on a roll of 5-6 on a d6 at the start of each turn. The damage scales dramatically with age:

| Age Category | Example (Red) | Breath DC | Damage |
|-------------|---------------|-----------|--------|
| Wyrmling | CR 4 | DC 13 | 7d6 fire |
| Young | CR 10 | DC 17 | 16d6 fire |
| Adult | CR 17 | DC 21 | 18d6 fire |
| Ancient | CR 24 | DC 24 | 26d6 fire |

Dragons are not merely monsters to be slain; they are forces of nature with centuries of accumulated #dragon-knowledge and magical power. A wise adventurer treats them with the respect they demand.
