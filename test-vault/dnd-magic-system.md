# D&D 5e Magic System

Magic in Dungeons & Dragons 5th Edition is governed by a structured system of spell slots, schools, and casting rules. Mastering these mechanics is key to playing any spellcasting class effectively.

## Spell Slots

Spell slots represent a caster's **magical energy** available each day. Slots are expended when casting spells and recovered after a long rest (with some exceptions like the Warlock's short rest recovery).

Key principles:
- Each slot has a **level** (1st through 9th)
- A spell can be cast using a slot of its level *or higher*, often with enhanced effects (called **upcasting**)
- Cantrips require no spell slots and can be cast indefinitely
- Multiclass characters calculate slots using a combined caster level table

### Spell Preparation

Prepared casters (Clerics, Druids, Paladins, Wizards) choose their spell list each day from all spells available to them. Known casters (Bards, Sorcerers, Rangers, Warlocks) have a fixed list that changes only on level-up.

## Schools of Magic

All spells belong to one of eight schools:

1. **Abjuration** - Protective magic: *Shield*, *Counterspell*, *Banishment*
2. **Conjuration** - Summoning and teleportation: *Misty Step*, *Conjure Animals*
3. **Divination** - Information gathering: *Detect Magic*, *Scrying*, *True Seeing*
4. **Enchantment** - Mind-affecting magic: *Charm Person*, *Hold Monster*
5. **Evocation** - Direct energy damage: *Fireball*, *Lightning Bolt*, *Healing Word*
6. **Illusion** - Deception and phantasms: *Minor Illusion*, *Major Image*
7. **Necromancy** - Life and death manipulation: *Animate Dead*, *Raise Dead*
8. **Transmutation** - Alteration of matter: *Polymorph*, *Haste*, *Stone Shape*

Wizard subclasses typically specialize in one school, gaining benefits when casting those spells.

## Concentration

Many powerful spells require **concentration** to maintain. The rules are strict:

- A caster can only concentrate on **one spell at a time**
- Taking damage requires a Constitution saving throw (DC 10 or half the damage, whichever is higher)
- Being incapacitated or killed ends concentration immediately
- Casting another concentration spell ends the current one

This mechanic prevents stacking multiple powerful buffs and creates meaningful tactical decisions. A wizard must choose between maintaining *Haste* on the fighter or casting *Wall of Force* to control the battlefield.

## Ritual Casting

Spells with the **ritual** tag can be cast without expending a spell slot by adding 10 minutes to the casting time. This is invaluable for utility spells like *Detect Magic*, *Identify*, and *Leomund's Tiny Hut*. Not all classes can ritual cast; it depends on class features.

## Components

Spells require up to three types of #spell-components:

- **Verbal (V)**: Spoken incantations
- **Somatic (S)**: Hand gestures (requires a free hand)
- **Material (M)**: Physical components, consumed or not, sometimes with a gold cost

A spellcasting focus can replace non-consumed, non-costed material components.
