# The Last Ember

*Draft - First Pass*

The dragon was old. Not old the way mountains are old, with their patient, grinding patience. Old the way a candle is old when the wick sputters and the wax pools thin across the saucer. She was burning out.

Vaelith lay curled in the remnants of her hoard, though *hoard* was too generous a word for what remained. A handful of coins so tarnished they no longer caught the light. A cracked sapphire. The jawbone of a knight who had been brave and foolish in equal measure, centuries ago. She kept the jawbone because she remembered his name --- Sir Aldric of the Northmarch --- and because no one else in the world did.

That was the burden of being the last. **Memory became obligation.**

She had watched the other dragons die. Not in battle, not in glory, but in slow diminishment. The magic was leaving the world like heat leaving a stone after sunset. Gradual. Inexorable. The great red wyrm Korthaxis had simply stopped breathing one winter, his fire finally spent. The silver Ilmaea had flown higher and higher one dawn until she vanished into the sun, choosing her own end rather than waiting for it.

Vaelith had chosen differently. She chose to *remember*.

Her cave overlooked a valley that had once held a kingdom. She could still see the outlines of roads in the grass, the depressions where foundations had stood. A river ran through it, oblivious to the civilizations it had nourished and outlived.

A sound reached her --- impossible, yet unmistakable. Footsteps. Small ones, hesitant, on the scree slope below her cave.

She opened one eye, its amber light dim but still present.

A child stood at the cave mouth. A girl, perhaps eight years old, clutching a book to her chest like a shield. She was shaking, but she did not run.

"Are you real?" the girl whispered.

Vaelith considered the question with the gravity it deserved. She was a creature of fading magic in a world that had forgotten magic existed. Was she real? Or was she a memory that had not yet realized it should stop?

"I am what remains," the dragon said. Her voice was smoke and gravel and the echo of something that had once shaken mountains.

The girl held out her book. "I found stories about you. About all of you. I came to ask ---" She faltered. "I came to ask if the stories are true."

The last ember in the dragon's chest flickered. Not brighter, exactly. But steadier.

"Sit down, child," Vaelith said. "And I will tell you which ones are."
